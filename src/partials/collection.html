  • In the Yavoriv district, the geometric ornament of the 19th century was replaced by a stylised floral one in the early 20th century.

    Its characteristic features are bright and embossed embroidery, different but balanced small motifs, with a lot of white space between them.

    It is how shirts, headdresses, camisoles and aprons were decorated, creating a light but bright image.

  • The textured embroidery of this shirt reproduces the ornamentation of the Chernihiv region, Nizhyn district, in the first half of the 20th century.

    Various machine embroidery techniques were used to imitate hand stitches.

    In the folk tradition, shirts "embroidered with oak" were common - the embroidery thread was dyed in a decoction of oak bark.

    An interesting technique is the punch needle one, known as "nightingale eyes", when it is used to make the entire element of the ornament.

  • The Odesa region is characterised by a combination of geometric ornaments of different structures and techniques.
